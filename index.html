What Makes Up a Basketball:

A basketball has 3 main components.

  1. Nylon Outside Wrapping
  2. Polyester Thread Inside Wrapping
  3. Butyl Rubber Bladder

Starting off with the outer wrapping, nylon, specifically nylon 6-6, has 2 strings of carbon, each 6 atoms long.

The polyester thread inside wrapping is made of ethylene glycol and adipi c acid. Ethlyene glycol has 2 hydrogen atoms and 2 oxygen atoms, and adipic acid has 2 hydrogen atoms and 4 oxygen atoms.

The butyl rubber bladder is a synthetic rubber made of isobutylene and isoprene. Isobutylene has 4 carbon atoms and 8 hydrogen atoms, while isoprene has 5 carbon atoms and 8 hydrogen atoms.
Together, they create butyl rubber, which has 9 carbon atoms and 16 hydrogen atoms.
